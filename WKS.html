Kannadigas in Wrocław 🇵🇱

A practical relocation guide for individuals and families from Karnataka

👋 Welcome

This page is for Kannada-speaking people planning to move or recently relocated to Wrocław, Poland. Whether you're coming alone for work or moving with your family, we’ve compiled essential info to make your transition easier.

📦 Relocating Alone

  • Visa & Work Permit: Usually arranged by employer (national visa D or EU Blue Card)
  • Temporary Stay: Can stay in hotels or short-term Airbnb until permanent flat is found
  • Housing: Sites like OLX, Otodom
  • PESEL Number: Not mandatory, but useful for tax and health services
  • Health Insurance: ZUS (public) or private like LuxMed/Medicover
  • Public Transport: Monthly pass ~110 PLN (check JakDojade)

👨‍👩‍👧 Relocating with Family

  • Dependent Visas: Apply for spouse and children under family reunification rules
  • Kindergartens & Schools: Public ones are free but Polish-speaking. Private international schools available (fee ~1500–3000 PLN/month)
  • Language: Basic Polish helps; consider weekend Polish classes
  • Family Health Insurance: Include dependents under your plan or buy private insurance
  • Local Community: Join Kannada/Indian groups on WhatsApp, Telegram, or Facebook for help and connections

📝 Legal Formalities

  • Register address at Urząd Miasta (City Office) within 30 days
  • Apply for Karta Pobytu (residence card) if staying >3 months
  • Tax registration if you're working—check with HR or use an accountant
  • Bank account setup requires passport + address confirmation
